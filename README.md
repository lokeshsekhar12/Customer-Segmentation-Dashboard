# Shopping Mall Customer Segmentation Dashboard

## Project Overview

This project presents an Excel-based Customer Segmentation Dashboard designed to analyse shopping mall customer behaviour based on demographic, income, and spending characteristics.

The dashboard transforms a dataset of over 15,000 customer records into clear visual insights, helping identify customer distribution patterns and key customer segments for business decision-making and targeted marketing strategies.

## Business Objective

The main objective of this project is to:

- Analyse customer demographics by gender and age group
- Understand customer income and spending behaviour
- Categorise customers into meaningful business segments
- Compare high-income and low-income customer groups
- Identify spending patterns across different customer profiles
- Present key findings through an interactive and visually structured Excel dashboard

## Dataset Overview

The dataset contains customer-level information with the following fields:

| Column | Description |
|---|---|
| Customer ID | Unique identifier assigned to each customer |
| Age | Customer age |
| Gender | Customer gender |
| Annual Income | Customer annual income |
| Spending Score | Customer spending behaviour score |
| Segment | Assigned customer segment |

The workbook contains more than 15,000 customer records used for segmentation and dashboard analysis.

## Customer Segmentation

Customers are classified into four business segments based on income and spending behaviour:

- High Income - High Spending
- High Income - Low Spending
- Low Income - High Spending
- Low Income - Low Spending

This segmentation framework helps businesses identify valuable customers, potential growth opportunities, and customer groups requiring different marketing approaches.

## Dashboard Features

The dashboard includes the following visualisations:

### Customer Gender Distribution
Displays the proportion of male and female customers in the dataset.

### Age Group Distribution
Shows customer distribution across the following age categories:

- 18-24
- 25-34
- 35-44
- 45-54
- 55-64
- 65+

### Customer Segment Distribution
Compares the number of customers across the four income and spending segments.

### Income vs Spending Analysis
Uses a scatter plot based on a sample of 1,000 customer records to visualise the relationship between annual income and spending score.

## Key Insights

- The customer base is almost evenly distributed between male and female customers.
- The 65+ age group represents the largest age category in the dataset.
- High Income - High Spending is the largest customer segment.
- Customer distribution across the four segments is relatively balanced.
- The income-versus-spending scatter analysis shows customers distributed across a wide range of annual income and spending scores.
- Customer segmentation enables businesses to design differentiated marketing and engagement strategies for specific customer groups.

## Business Recommendations

Based on the segmentation analysis:

- Retain High Income - High Spending customers through loyalty programmes, premium offers, and personalised engagement.
- Increase engagement with High Income - Low Spending customers through targeted promotions and product recommendations.
- Maintain value-focused campaigns for Low Income - High Spending customers while monitoring price sensitivity.
- Use discounts, bundled offers, and awareness campaigns to improve engagement among Low Income - Low Spending customers.
- Develop age-specific marketing strategies based on customer distribution patterns.
